# The Maritime Republic of Aurivale

## Description
A nation built on trade routes and merchant power, Aurivale spans a strategic archipelago controlling key shipping lanes. Golden harbors bustle with vessels from across the world, while merchant palaces line canals that serve as streets. Success is measured in coins and contracts, with even nobility determined by wealth rather than birth.

## Organization
A plutocratic republic governed by the Grand Exchange - a council of the wealthiest merchant houses. Leadership rotates annually between council members, with the title of First Merchant bestowed upon the chair. Laws and policies are voted upon based on shares owned in the national trade company, effectively allowing the richest houses to control legislation.

## Powerful Entities
- The Grand Exchange: Ruling council of merchant princes
- The Golden Scale Bank: Controls currency and loans
- Navigator's Guild: Maintains maps and trade routes
- The Harbor Watch: Combined navy and customs force
- House of Contracts: Legal institution enforcing trade agreements
- The Foreign Quarter Consortium: Representatives of foreign traders

## Important People
- First Merchant Dario Valetti: Current head of state
- Isabella Morneau: Director of the Golden Scale Bank
- Captain General Feng Wei: Commander of Harbor Watch
- Arbitrator Supreme Giulia Romano: Chief of House of Contracts
- Trade Master Lars Erikson: Head of Foreign Quarter Consortium
- Fleet Admiral Sophia Drake: Commander of merchant marine

## Marginalized Entities
- The Indentured: Those who fell into debt slavery
- Dock Workers: Manual laborers with few rights
- Island Natives: Original inhabitants pushed to poorest districts
- Failed Merchants: Bankrupted traders and their families
- Unlicensed Craftsmen: Those who can't afford guild memberships
- The Coinless: Absolute poor who can't participate in commerce

## Important Marginalized People
- Kaz the Chainbreaker: Leader of indentured workers' underground
- Chief Mako: Elder of displaced island natives
- Rosa the Crafty: Spokesperson for unlicensed artisans
- Captain James Broke: Famous failed merchant turned folk hero
- Marina the Snake: Queen of dock worker gangs
- Old Man Chen: Keeper of native traditions and stories
