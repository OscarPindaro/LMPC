# The Holy Kingdom of Luminara

## Description
Luminara is a kingdom where secular and religious power blend seamlessly, founded upon the belief that their first king was directly chosen by the deity Solaris to lead humanity. The kingdom's architecture reflects this divine connection, with towering cathedrals and golden spires that catch the sunlight. Cities are built around temples, and religious ceremonies mark every significant political event. The kingdom's legitimacy rests entirely on maintaining the perception of divine favor.

## Organization
The political structure is a hereditary monarchy, but with a unique dual-power system. The King or Queen must be crowned by the High Luminary (religious leader) to be considered legitimate. Major decisions require the approval of both the Crown Council (secular) and the Solar Conclave (religious). The monarchy handles earthly matters like trade, warfare, and justice, while the church maintains spiritual authority, education, and determines if royal decrees align with divine will.

## Powerful Entities
- The Order of Solar Truth: The state church, which controls education and maintains religious orthodoxy
- The Crown Council: Noble advisors who manage day-to-day governance
- The Illuminated Treasury: A joint secular-religious institution controlling state finances
- The Knights of Divine Light: Elite military order answering to both crown and church
- The Circle of Oracles: Religious seers who interpret divine will and prophecy

## Important People
- King Aldrich IV: Current monarch, struggling to balance church influence with royal authority
- High Luminary Celestine: Religious leader, known for her political cunning
- Duke Magnus Blackthorn: Head of Crown Council, secret skeptic of divine right
- Oracle Seraphina: Most influential prophet, whose visions shape policy
- Archbishop Roland: Ambitious church official challenging both king and High Luminary
- Lady Beatrice: Royal spymaster who knows everyone's secrets

## Marginalized Entities
- The Unblessed: Those deemed to lack divine favor, often marked by physical differences
- Followers of Old Ways: Practitioners of pre-Solaris traditions
- The Shadow Cast: Those born during solar eclipses, considered ill-omened
- Foreign Merchants: Treated with suspicion and heavily taxed
- Scientific Scholars: Those who seek natural explanations for phenomena
- The Lightless: Poor urban dwellers who can't afford temple tithes

## Important Marginalized People
- Helena the Wise: Underground leader of scientific scholars
- Grimwald: Charismatic leader of old ways practitioners
- Sister Maria: Defrocked priestess who now aids the Unblessed
- Dante the Shadow: Born during an eclipse, now a powerful underground figure
- Professor Marcus Vale: Exiled scientist gathering followers in secret
- Lyra the Lightless: Charismatic speaker for poor urban communities
