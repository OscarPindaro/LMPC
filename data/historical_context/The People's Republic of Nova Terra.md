# The People's Republic of Nova Terra

## Description
A newly established socialist state born from a workers' revolution, Nova Terra is experiencing the turbulent transition from its previous oligarchic system. Factory complexes and communal housing projects dominate the landscape, while former luxury mansions are being converted into public institutions. The nation vibrates with revolutionary energy, though the practical implementation of marxist ideals faces daily challenges.

## Organization
A system of nested workers' councils (soviets) forms the backbone of governance. Local councils elect representatives to regional councils, which in turn send delegates to the Supreme Soviet. The Communist Party maintains significant influence but faces internal debates between various ideological factions. All major industries are in the process of nationalization, with workers' committees taking control of management.

## Powerful Entities
- The Supreme Soviet: Highest legislative body
- The Communist Party Central Committee: Primary policy-making body
- The People's Defense Force: Military and militia units
- The State Planning Commission: Directs economic policy
- The Workers' Trade Union Congress: Organizes labor
- The Revolutionary Youth League: Political education and mobilization

## Important People
- Chairperson Elena Martinez: Head of Supreme Soviet
- General Secretary Thomas Reid: Communist Party leader
- Commander Sofia Petrov: Head of People's Defense
- Director Wei Chen: Chief Economic Planner
- Union Leader Marcus Brown: Head of Trade Congress
- Professor Sarah Dubois: Leading party theorist

## Marginalized Entities
- Former Business Owners: Labeled as class enemies
- Religious Practitioners: Viewed with suspicion as "opium of the masses"
- Academic "Traditionalists": Those resisting marxist interpretation
- Non-Party Professionals: Experts without party membership
- Rural Landowners: Resisting agricultural collectivization
- "Reactionary" Artists: Those not embracing socialist realism

## Important Marginalized People
- Doctor Alexander White: Leading non-party intellectual
- Father Michael O'Connor: Underground religious leader
- Professor James Harper: Advocate for academic freedom
- Maria Garcia: Former factory owner turned dissident
- Artist Vincent Chen: Champion of artistic freedom
- Anna Volkov: Leader of rural resistance to collectivization
